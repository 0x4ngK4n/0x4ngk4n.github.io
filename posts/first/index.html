A little About me…

I am Angkan and as of today, I have been in IT for about 14 years now.

Back when I started, I was doing tech support for firewalls which, while I didn’t enjoy much but got me hooked into how computers communicate. Fast forward few job hoops where I acquired skills such as scripting and nix fundamentals, I landed a role where I gained exposure to first hand cyber-attacks employed by attackers-in-the-wild. This was 10 years back from today. Attacks were mostly around lack of input validation, source to sink based, deterministic in nature and like many before me and after, I was also like wow ^~^ would be cool to be a hacker-at-work.

Consulting was mostly nice as I got to work on web, mobile apps as well as thick clients. Most importantly, what I learnt was how to document the findings to professionally relay to the clients. More often than not, this is a really important part of overall project delivery. Then from a general web app consultant, I landed roles as a red teamer and maldev where I got to work with some of the very best in the industry while actually executing entire cyber kill chains. THIS WAS FUN!

However, while maldev wasis cool and I really loved red teaming, what always had got me excited was initial access. Specially zero or 1 click exploits. With some experience over the years and being a generalist in various domains in cyber-sec, I thought it was high time I specialise in one skill set and be really excellent at that.

Especially with advent and improvements around AI, where we are at the cusp of interfacing with computers just with human language (yet another higher level abstraction over high level computer languages), not a better time to get closer to the machine and hardware to keep that professional edge.

This blog will detail my journey towards an N-day researcher. It won’t just start with N-days but more like, revising and learning new concepts around binary exploitation, getting hands dirty on wargames, CTFs and finally to N-day CVE analysis and crafting PoCs but most importanly documenting my thought process.

Lets go!
